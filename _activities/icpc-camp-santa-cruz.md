, from May 25 to May 29, with the purpose of bringing more university/college students into the Competitive Programming World, and having me as an invited learner (I was the only participant who was eligible for IOI).

Even though I already knew most of the content covered here, I believe that it's important to go back to basics, because maybe something needed a bit more reviewing.

I will be talking about what I took from each contest. The contests were ICPC-style, where we had a printed copy of all the problems available, 5 hours for each contest, and 5 hours learning the topics in the morning


## Day 1: Intro day

Since it was the first class, the content covered here was basic, I only couldn't solve one problem this day, mostly because of time, which had an interesting math focus.

<img src="/assets/img/ICPC-day1.png" class="center">

## Day 2: Recursion, Backtracking, and Bitmasks

I had to review some things about bitmasks, so this day was very helpful. I could AK (All-Kill), meaning solve everything, in this day's contest, having many classic problems.

<img src="/assets/img/ICPC-day2.png" class="center">

## Day 3: Dynamic Programming (DP)

DP is a difficult topic for me, so reviewing some basic problems again was helpful. Even though I had reviewed the problems we learnt in class previously (for the IOI qualification), it was helpful to see other ways to solve them.

I AKd the contest again.

<img src="/assets/img/ICPC-day3.png" class="center">

## Day 4: Graphs

We covered the basic things related to graphs, such as traversal, representation, components, and Minimum Spanning Trees.

The contest had other topics, though; for example, problem K was a mix of graphs, bitmasks, and DP, while problem C was a math problem disguised as a graph.  
I couldn't solve 4 of them, but after a brief explanation from Pacha, I understood the idea behind the solution and solved them.

<img src="/assets/img/ICPC-day4.png" class="center">

## Day 5: Data Structures, Binary Search, & Number Theory

For me, the most useful topic was number theory, since many interesting tricks appear often in problems.

Since this was the last day, It had a bit of every topic we covered, with the hardest problems being solved with DP on trees.

<img src="/assets/img/ICPC-day5.png" class="center">

## Conclusions

Thanks to the camp, I was able to review a lot of important material. Even though I had already gone over some of the topics before, the book that Pacha lent me (How to Solve It by Pólya) allowed me to keep learning at all times.

Thanks, Pacha.
